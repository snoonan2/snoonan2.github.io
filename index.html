Projects & Leadership:

CS for Good is the largest Computer Science organization on campus, focusing on giving back to the local South Bend community. As the Communications and Events Director, I coordinate with non-profits and volunteer organizations to plan projects that can be proposed to computer science Notre Dame student teams to solve a technical issue using a wide set of programming knowledge. I am also responsible of the club's social medias, contacts, recruitment campaigns, and branding. These designs are created through graphic design platforms: Canva, AdobeSpark, and photo editors. As an Executive Board Member of Women in Computer Science, a club founded on the empowerment of female students on the Computer Science path at Notre Dame, I strive to promote power-feminity in and out of the classroom. WICS offers a community of strong women to encourage and mentor other members and girls in surround South Bend area with weekly volunteer work and monthly club meetings. After petiotioning to be as an offical club, Women in Computer Science will now be recognized as an offical club at the University of Notre Dame in the fall of 2024!

University of Minnesota CSE Research - Natural Language Processing Lab

Under the guidance of Professor Maria Gini, I researched current developments in Machine Learning and User-Robot interaction for bi-weekly reports to a graduate student team for advice on new language processing algorithm techniques and application methods. By applying new ML techniques, our team was able to improve accuracy of the lab's robot vision sensor functionality through Python based testing for identifying common grocery store objects with the lab objective to design a user-friendly visual impairment application for blind students on campus. Debugging code, generating personalities for individual user's bots to operate with, and testing the device, I had the opportunity to be an active participant in the Robot-Human Interaction academic field.

Amazon Prime Video SIBC Travel Team

After being selected to be a Travel Team Member for the Amazon Student International Business Council Project, our team of seven Notre Dame student researched to improve Amazon Prime Video's market share in the Video Subscription On-Demand Industry. Traveling to Amazon HQ in Seattle, WA, we presented findings to a board of Prime Video Marketing executives and video editing engineers on technical solutions. The team designed social media campaigns and visual data analysis. I personally presented on my mock ups for a new live streaming e-commerce feature - adding to cart while viewing - that is now within Prime Original series and movies in the Summer of 2023.
