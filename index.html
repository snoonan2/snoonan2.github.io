About Me!

My name is Sophia Noonan, a Junior pursuing a major in Computer Science in College of Engineering with a minor in Mandarin Chinese at the University of Notre Dame - go irish! My academic journey is driven by a profound passion for ethical technology and its positive transformative potential for our modern world. Beyond my academic pursuits, I am an ardent enthusiast of diverse interests and creative outlets. As an avid follower of fashion history & media, I enjoy analyzing style trends local and internationally. When not at games cheering on the Fighting Irish or walking around the lakes, I find my "me time" through pilates and yoga.

Projects & Leadership:

CS for Good is the largest Computer Science organization on campus, focusing on giving back to the local South Bend community. As the Communications and Events Director, I coordinate with non-profits and volunteer organizations to plan projects that can be proposed to computer science Notre Dame student teams to solve a technical issue using a wide set of programming knowledge. I am also responsible of the club’s social medias, contacts, recruitment campaigns, and branding. These designs are created through graphic design platforms: Canva, AdobeSpark, and photo editors. With the current active membership of roughly 450 students, organizing and designing events such as Interview Prep, Speakers, and Live Demo Workshops as the Events Director, I strive to keep the club an engaged, dedicated, and growing community.

As an Executive Board Member of Women in Computer Science, a club founded on the empowerment of female students on the Computer Science path at Notre Dame, I strive to promote power-feminity in and out of the classroom. WICS offers a community of strong women to encourage and mentor other members and girls in surround South Bend area with weekly volunteer work and monthly club meetings. As the Apparel Coordinator, I design clothing, stickers, and other merchendise to promote Women in Computer Science around campus and beyond. After petiotioning to be as an offical club, Women in Computer Science will now be recognized as an offical club at the University of Notre Dame in the fall of 2024!

Starting as applying to be a Model Creative on a whim my sophmore year - hoping to gain confidence in joining a publication I loved on campus - I have now come to be one of the Fashion Directors as a Booking Director of Notre Dame's chapter of Strike Magazine. Interviewing model applications, coordinating fittings, organizing six shoots while adapting on the fly. Quickly hemming a dress or sewing one of our models into a South Bend thrifted piece, the role of Fashion Director has let me express my passions outside of engineering while improving my problem solving capabilities. Strike focuses on culture, fashion, and art and aims to cultivate creative communities on college campuses by allowing students to gain 360 professional experience in all facets of content production. Strike is the largest student-run publication in the nation with Notre Dame being one of 12 current chapters. Strike strive to embrace the unique attributes of all individuals and yield a deep appreciation for fashion, art, and culture.

University of Minnesota CSE Research - Natural Language Processing Lab

Under the guidance of Professor Maria Gini, I researched current developments in Machine Learning and User-Robot interaction for bi-weekly reports to a graduate student team for advice on new language processing algorithm techniques and application methods. By applying new ML techniques, our team was able to improve accuracy of the lab's robot vision sensor functionality through Python based testing for identifying common grocery store objects with the lab objective to design a user-friendly visual impairment application for blind students on campus. Debugging code, generating personalities for individual user's bots to operate with, and testing the device, I had the opportunity to be an active participant in the Robot-Human Interaction academic field.
